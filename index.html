The Step BEFORE You Invest In or Patent a New Technology

"Anything that won’t sell, I don’t want to invent."

Thomas Edison

Will It Sell?

Thomas Edison found this out the hard way. It doesn't matter how great a new technology is if no one will buy it.

Should You Get A Patent?

Not all novel inventions will sell. Not all innovations need a patent. Knowing what to patent and what to keep secret can be a big competitive advantage against your competitors.

What Should The Patent Focus On?

Since the priority date is from the date of filing, we want you to have priority on the most valuable features from the earliest date possible.

What Are Early Exits?

Not all new companies exit through an IPO. You want to know how to cash out so you can benefit from your hard work. You also want to know what you need to do to make those early exits and option.

Patent Portfolio Enhancement

Are you spending a huge portion of your budget filing patents to protect your innovation but you are still not sure if the valuable features are protected? Are you worried that you spent all of that money and your competitors will still develop a work-around to compete with you?

Technology Development Assessment

Is your burn rate sustainable if you launch your technology and no one buys it? Are you thinking about putting your time and life savings into developing an innovation but are not sure if it will be a flop or a phenominal success?

Investment Opportunity Assessment

Do you have the opportunity to invest in an early stage company or a new technology but you don't know what questions to ask? Are you willing to risk your money on your gut feeling or theoretical financial projections?

Our Mission

To help you make the right decision while you still have resources to change your strategy. That might mean giving you clear evidence that you should stop working on the project. An early "No" can save you an enormous amount of time and money.

Our Philosophy

“Anything that won’t sell, I don’t want to invent. Its sale is proof of utility, and utility is success.” - Thomas Edison

Everything we do is to make sure you are spending your time and money on what sells.

Story Behind ZEROTH

I have watched too many inventors, companies, and investors put their time and money into innovations that will not sell. Both large companies and single inventors burn through large sums of money filing for patent after patent. Ultimately, most of those expenses will never be recovered. ZEROTH was created to help innovators and investors verify if new technology will sell and how they should focus their time and money developing it.
